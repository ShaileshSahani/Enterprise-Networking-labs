🔧 Lab 02/50 — PVST+ Per-VLAN Load Balancing

Built a redundant Layer 2 campus topology using PVST+ to analyze per-VLAN traffic forwarding and STP convergence behavior.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Topology:
• 2 Distribution switches
• 3 Access switches
• VLANs 10, 20, 30
• Dual uplinks
• PVST+ enabled throughout the network

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Objectives:
• Observe default STP elections
• Manipulate Root Bridge placement
• Validate per-VLAN forwarding paths
• Analyze blocked-port behavior
• Test reconvergence during link failure

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Configuration:
• DSW1 configured as Root Primary for VLAN 10
• DSW2 configured as Root Primary for VLAN 20

Result:
Different VLANs selected different forwarding paths through the topology.

This demonstrates how PVST+ can distribute Layer 2 traffic across redundant links instead of relying on a single forwarding path.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Failure Testing:

An uplink was intentionally disconnected to observe STP reconvergence behavior.

Observed:
• Root ports recalculated
• Blocking ports transitioned
• Traffic rerouted automatically
• Temporary packet drops during convergence
• Stable forwarding restored after recalculation

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Verification Performed:
• show spanning-tree vlan
• Root bridge validation
• Port role analysis
• Trunk verification
• Continuous ping testing
• Topology reconvergence observation

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This lab focused on:
