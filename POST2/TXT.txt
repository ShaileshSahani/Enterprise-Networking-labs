Lab 02/50 — PVST+ Per-VLAN Load Balancing

Built a redundant Layer 2 campus topology with:

• 2 Distribution switches
• 3 Access switches
• VLANs 10/20/30
• Dual uplinks
• PVST+

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

What I tested:

• Natural STP root election
• Manual root bridge manipulation
• Different forwarding paths per VLAN
• STP blocked-port movement
• Link failure reconvergence

Configured:
• DSW1 as root for VLAN 10
• DSW2 as root for VLAN 20

Result:

Different VLANs used different physical paths through the network.

Exactly how real enterprise Layer 2 load balancing works.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Most tutorials explain STP like this:

“STP prevents loops.”

But the important part is:
Which links actually forward traffic after convergence?

That’s where this lab gets interesting.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Then I broke an uplink intentionally.

Watched:
• STP recalculate topology
• Root ports change
• Blocked ports transition
• Traffic reroute automatically

A few dropped pings during convergence.
Then stable again.
