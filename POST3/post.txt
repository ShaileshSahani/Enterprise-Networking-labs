🔧 Lab 03/50 — RSTP vs Legacy STP Convergence

Today’s objective was simple:

Break the network and measure how quickly it recovers.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Built a redundant Layer 2 topology with:
• 1 Distribution switch
• 2 Access switches
• Redundant uplinks
• VLAN 10
• Continuous traffic testing

Then compared:
• Legacy 802.1D STP
vs
• Rapid Spanning Tree Protocol (RSTP)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Phase 1 — Legacy STP Testing

The topology initially operated using classic 802.1D STP.

An active uplink was disconnected intentionally during continuous ping testing.

Observed:
• ~30+ seconds of packet loss
• Slow topology recalculation
• Port transitions through:

* Listening
* Learning
* Forwarding

This demonstrated why traditional STP convergence can significantly impact production traffic during failures.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Phase 2 — RSTP Testing

The same topology was migrated to RSTP and the identical uplink failure was repeated.

Observed:
• Near-immediate traffic recovery
• Only a few dropped pings during reconvergence
• Rapid port role/state transitions
• Faster restoration of forwarding paths

The difference in recovery time was substantial.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Most networking tutorials simply state:
“RSTP is faster than STP.”

This lab focused on:
How convergence speed directly affects live traffic during real Layer 2 failures.
