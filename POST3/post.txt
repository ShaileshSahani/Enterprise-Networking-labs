Lab 03/50 — RSTP vs Legacy STP Convergence

Today’s lab was simple:

Break the network and measure how fast it recovers.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Built a redundant Layer 2 topology with:

• 1 Distribution switch
• 2 Access switches
• Redundant uplinks
• VLAN 10
• Continuous traffic testing

Then compared:

• Legacy 802.1D STP
VS
• Rapid STP (RSTP)

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

First test:

One switch running classic STP.

Pulled the active uplink.

Result:
~30+ seconds of packet loss before traffic recovered.

Watched the port move through:
• Listening
• Learning
• Forwarding

Exactly why legacy STP feels painfully slow in production.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Second test:

Converted the topology to RSTP.

Repeated the same link failure.

Result:
Traffic recovered almost immediately.

Only a couple dropped pings during convergence.

Huge difference.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Most people memorize:
“RSTP is faster.”

This lab shows:
HOW much faster.

And more importantly:
What that actually looks like during a real failure.
