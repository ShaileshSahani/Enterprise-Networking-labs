Lab 04/50 — UplinkFast & BackboneFast

Today’s lab was about one thing:

How much downtime classic STP causes during failures.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Built a redundant Layer 2 topology with:

• Distribution switch
• Dual access switches
• Redundant uplinks
• Legacy 802.1D STP

Then intentionally started breaking links.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

First test:
Default STP behavior.

Pulled the active uplink.

Result:
~30–50 seconds of outage before traffic recovered.

Watched ports move through:
• Blocking
• Listening
• Learning
• Forwarding

Painfully slow.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Then enabled:
• UplinkFast
• BackboneFast

Repeated the same failures.

Huge difference.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

UplinkFast:
Backup uplink transitioned almost immediately.

BackboneFast:
Indirect failures were detected much faster instead of waiting for timers to expire.

Much less packet loss during convergence.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Most people only learn:
“RSTP is faster.”

But older enterprise networks relied heavily on:
• UplinkFast
• BackboneFast
• PortFast

to survive STP outages before Rapid STP became standard.
