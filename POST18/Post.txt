🔥 OSPF Stub Area Design & Route Filtering

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Topology:

• R1 → ASBR
• R2 → ABR
• R3 → Internal Area Router

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Initial State:

Area 1 was configured as a normal OSPF area.

R1 redistributed connected routes into OSPF, and R3 learned external routes normally.

Verified using:

show ip route ospf

Observed:

• O E2 external routes present in Area 1

Then the real lab started.

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 1 — Convert Area 1 into Stub Area

Configured:

area 1 stub

Result:

✅ External Type-5 LSAs blocked
✅ External routes disappeared
✅ Default route injected automatically by the ABR

Routing table became much smaller and cleaner.

Verified using:

show ip route ospf

Instead of learning multiple external routes, R3 now only received:

O*IA 0.0.0.0/0

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Step 2 — Convert Area 1 into Totally Stubby Area

Configured on ABR:

area 1 stub no-summary

Result:

✅ External routes removed
✅ Inter-area summary routes removed
✅ Only a default route remained

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

This lab demonstrated how enterprises reduce:

• Routing table size
• SPF calculations
• CPU utilization
• LSDB complexity

━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

Key Learning:

Branch routers do not need thousands of enterprise routes.

In real enterprise WAN environments, remote sites often only need:

0.0.0.0/0 → toward HQ or Datacenter

That is exactly why Stub and Totally Stubby Areas exist.
